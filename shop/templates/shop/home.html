FREE THE IMAGINATION

It’s hard to avoid the onslaught of electronic toys.
Walk down the toy aisles of most department stores,
and you’re likely to find a lineup of playthings that sing,
move, light up or synch with a tablet.
There are teapots that sing, books with sound effects, remote control cars,
toy phones and laptops, robots, motorized race-car tracks
and electronic musical instruments.

The problem is, playing with electronic toys is more often a passive activity -
the more the toy can do, the less the child can do.
With screens and electronics so present in kids’ lives,

it's time to bring back classic toys.

Interacting with simple, classic toys can provide
infinite opportunities for learning and development.

Traditional toys tend to give the children immediate feedback,
and they can adjust to that feedback.

With kids imaginations set free, they become sharks and turtles.
They travel to Mexico and across Japan.
Their stuffies develop identities and have families of their own.
They make forts under the table.
They read.
They play card games and strategy games.
They explore and solve problems.

Children are inherently capable if only they're encouraged to explore.
